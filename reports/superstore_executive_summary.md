## Superstore Performance Analysis - Executive Summary

### Situation

This analysis investigated product categories, regions, customer segments, and discounting practices across 9,994 orders from 2014 to 2017. Using exploratory data analysis, SQL quries, and a Tableau dashboard, we set out to identify which areas are dragging down overall profit margins - and give leadership a clear picture of where to act.

### Key Findings

- Furniture is the least profitable category at just 2% profit margin, with Central region Furniture being the only loss-making region-category combination at -$2,871.

- Discounts above 20% are a danger zone - 933 orders with discounts above 40% generated a combined loss of $99k. Office Supplies in Central region is the single biggest discount-driven loss at -$33k.

- The Cubify CubeX 3D Printer is the worst performing individual product at -$9k loss, appearing in the bottom 10 most loss-making products.

- All three customer segments operate on thin profit margins of around 11-14%. Home office has the highest margin at 14% despite the lowest sales volume.

- Sales and profit both grew from 2014 to 2017, with profit margin improving from 10% to 13% - indicating the business is becoming more efficient over time.

### Recommendations

1. Review and cap discount at 20% - 933 orders with discounts above 40% generated a combines loss of $99k. Reducing heavy discounting, particularly in Office Supplies Central which lost %33 from high-discount orders alone, could significantly recover profit margins.

2. Investigate and address underperforming products - the bottom 10 products generated a combined loss of $27k. Products like the Cubify CubeX 3D Printer (-$9k) should be reviewd for discontinuation or repricing before the next sales cycle.

3. Grow investment in high-margin areas - Home Office has the highest profit margin at 14% despite the lowest sales volume, suggesting untapped potential. Technology in East ($47k profit) and Office Supplies in West ($53k profit) are the strongest performing combinations and warrant increased sales focus.

### Risks & Caveats

This analysis identifies what is underperforming but does not always explain why - for example, whether the Cubify CubeX's losses are driven by heavy discounting or low demand. Additionally, the data covers 2014-2017 only. Market conditions have changed significantly since then - categories like 3D printing that appeared loss-making may now be more viable, and recommendations should be validated against current data before action is taken.
